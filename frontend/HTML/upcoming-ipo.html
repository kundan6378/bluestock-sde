The IPO start date is announced by the issuing company and is available on their website or official exchange platforms like NSE/BSE. You can also find this information through your broker or financial news portals.

Issue size refers to the total value of shares offered in the IPO. It is calculated by multiplying the total number of shares offered by the issue price.

The number of shares in a lot is determined by the company and varies for each IPO. The lot size is mentioned in the IPO details provided by the company or exchange.

Retail investors can participate in most IPOs, but some IPOs are restricted to institutional investors or high-net-worth individuals. Check the IPO details for eligibility criteria.

If an IPO is oversubscribed, the shares are allocated based on a lottery system or proportionally, depending on the rules set by the issuing company or regulators.
